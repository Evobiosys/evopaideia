Why a named vocabulary

A habit you can name is a habit you can practise, score, and carry across domains

The founder studied at Minerva University, whose curriculum demonstrated something rare in education: that a finite, named vocabulary of thinking-moves can be explicitly taught in one foundational year, scored every time a learner applies one, and then tracked as those moves transfer — near and far — into every later context. The habit, not the course, becomes the unit of learning.

EvoPaideia keeps that structural insight and re-derives the vocabulary from a different root. Minerva's habits cover analytical and communicative intelligence — roughly one third of what a whole human being needs. The EvoSattva Habits grow instead from the three qualities the curriculum exists to cultivate:

◎

Polymath

Polymathic Intelligence — sense-making habits: patterns, evidence, systems, dialectics.

○

Bodhisattva

Interoperable Compassion — relational and inner habits: attunement, perspective, shared fields, shadow.

●

World-Creator

World-Creation Agency — enactment habits: vision, co-creation, shipping.

An Evo-sattva — an evolving bodhisattva — thinks like a polymath, relates like a bodhisattva, and builds like a world-creator. The twelve habits make that aspiration practicable.

The Dialectical Meta-Layer

Four movements of thought that deepen every habit — after Otto Laske

Above the twelve habits sits one more layer — not a thirteenth habit but a way of moving through all of them. It draws directly on Otto Laske's dialectical thought forms: four classes of movement that distinguish thinking about a fixed world from thinking with a world in transformation.

Process Prozess

What is this in the middle of becoming? Emergence and unceasing change.

Context Kontext

What larger structure holds this? Embeddedness and stable form.

Relationship Beziehung

What does this share ground with? Interdependence and common ground.

Transformation Transformation

How does the whole system change — including its limits? Development itself.

In practice: any habit, applied to any material, can be pushed through the four movements — and the fluidity of that movement is the developmental signal. The same analysis performed logically earns mastery; performed dialectically it earns depth. Daily MindOpener prompts train exactly one movement at a time: “you have described the structure — now show what it is becoming.”

This layer is what makes EvoPaideia post-conventional rather than better-conventional: critical thinking taught at the formal-logical altitude sharpens the mind inside its frame; dialectical practice teaches the frame to move.

How the Habits Travel

Introduced once — then scored wherever life applies them

Each habit is introduced in the foundational year, then re-scored every time it shows up in later work — the farther from its home domain, the more it counts. Assessment matures over the whole arc rather than closing at the end of a course: the habit vocabulary is the spine, and each container of the container ladder is a new context in which the same twelve habits are practised at greater depth.

Container · the year on

One year — sabbatical, gap year, year on

The First-Patterns year standing alone as a complete experience: all twelve habits introduced, practised, and taken home. A year on rather than a year off — for gap-year students, sabbatical professionals, or credit-integration into an existing degree. This is the proving container: the four-year journey opens only after the year on has held.

Container · the four-year journey

Four years — the full arc

Year one lays the foundation with the twelve habits. Years two and three follow desire-led learning — live projects and developmental fields in which the same habits are scored at growing distance and depth. Year four turns to the world: a capstone carried to reality, and the accumulated learning mapped toward an accreditable degree with partner universities — desire first, degree second.

And the AI question: AI-assisted engineering — vibecoding, agentic toolchains, digital fabrication — runs through both containers as a flagship application set: a domain the modes of thinking support creating in, not a discipline of its own. Co-Creation with AI and Idea to Life are the habits; this decade's toolchain is merely their current instrument. When the tools are replaced, the habits transfer.

Where This Stands

Deliberately early — a seed vocabulary, stated plainly

  • Real: the twelve-habit seed set (v0.2), derived from the EvoSattva triad and refined against the founder's first-hand experience of Minerva's habit system.
  • Real: the dialectical meta-layer, grounded in Laske's published framework, with a daily practice format designed.
  • Not yet: no cohort has practised this vocabulary. No rubrics are written. The assessment instrument mix remains undecided.
  • Expected: the vocabulary will change. Names will be dropped, split, and added once real learners work with them — that is the point of calling it a seed set.
In the spirit of the pitch: this page makes no claim it cannot stand behind. The habits are a design, not yet a result.
